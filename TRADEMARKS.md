- Use the Marks in truthful, descriptive references — for example "a fork of ArtifactFlow",

- Keep the existing ArtifactFlow name and branding in an **unmodified** redistribution.

## Please do not (without written permission)

- Use the Marks, or a confusingly similar name or logo, as the name of a **modified** version, a
  fork, a hosted service, or a product, in a way that implies it is the official ArtifactFlow or is

  confuse people about the source of the software.
- Alter the Marks or present them as your own.

If you distribute a modified version, please give it a different name and make clear that it is
**derived from, but not the official,** ArtifactFlow.

## Why this exists

The AGPL keeps the *code* free for everyone. This policy keeps the *name* meaningful, so users can
tell the official project from forks and can't be misled about who stands behind a given build. It
follows the spirit of established open-source trademark policies (e.g. the Mozilla and Django
models).
